Overview

The evolution of the Universe on small scales is non-linear. This can be seen as both a blessing and a curse. While non-linear effects offer a handle to break parameter degeneracies, their theoretical modeling is highly challenging and uncertain. The many approaches to deal with the complications of non-linear evolution can be roughly grouped into two categories: While some try to model the fully non-linear observations, others aim at undoing the non-linear effects and reconstructing the well understood linear phenomena. Both approaches go hand in hand with numerical simulations.

The aim of this workshop organized by the Berkeley Center for Cosmological Physics (BCCP) is to illuminate both of these sides by bringing together experts for reconstruction, non-linear modeling and simulations. The format is short talks and extended discussion sessions, promoting interactions between cosmologists at all career-stages.

Where: Hotel San Martin, Smartno, Slovenia
When: July 15-21, 2018

Topics will include BAO reconstruction, Bayesian reconstruction techniques, numerical simulations, theoretical modeling of non-linear effects, and connections to observations.

Venue

The conference will take place in the medieval village of Smartno in Slovenia, on the green hills of the region of Brda(Collio), right at the border with Italy. This is one of the most important and renowned wine growing areas of the region.
The venue is located in the Hotel San Martin, where a large fraction of the participants will also find accommodation.
Additional events, such as wine tasting and excursion the sea side and the Julian Alps will be organized. Further information will be made available soon on the accomodation page.
